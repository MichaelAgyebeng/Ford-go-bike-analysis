Ford Go Bike Data Exploration¶

by Michael Owusu Agyebeng¶

Investigation Overview¶

This data set is taken from https://www.fordgobike.com/system-data and represents trips taken by members of the service for month of February of 2019. I aim at exploring the variables that influence duration of these trips.

What's the distribution of Duration of trips?¶

Duration is a long-tailed right skewed distribution, with a lot of rides on the fewer minutes end, and close to none on the further right side. When plotted on a log-scale, the duration distribution still looks right skewed, with the peak between 0 and 100 minutes and also some increment between 1000 and 1200 minutes.

Next up, the first predictor variable of interest: Gender.

What is the share of Gender in the data?¶

In this case, gender was plotted on a pie chart and it revealed that almost 75% of the members were males, 23% females and 2% identified as another gender

Next up, the other predictor variable of interest: User Type.

What is the percentage of Subscribers and Customers who went on trips?¶

90.5% of the users are subsribers with only 9.5% being customers

Relationship between age and Duration¶

From the plot, there is no clear relationship between Age and Duration of trips/ rides except that the graph tells us majority of the riders were young and almost all of them went for very short duration trips i.e, less than 30 minutes

Highest times during the day user types go out for trips¶

It is obvious More subscribers went on rides than customers, also the distribution of the customers mimicked that of the subsribers in the senses that they also got peak rides at 8:00 and 17:00 and decreased when they decreased

Gender and the times they go for bike rides¶

Similarly, Male and Female distributions looked alike except for the huge variation in the number of rides by males

Gender daily duration¶

Averagely, the Other gender had more duration for each day than Males and Females. This I believe is as a result of the huge number of males and females with a lot of the going for shorter durations. Also, there is a spike in duration for other and females on the weekends

Gender Hourly duration¶

Averagely, the longest lasting trips started at 2:00 am and 3:00 am with the other being showing its dominance. Also, Males average duration was particularly very stable and was the least at every time

Conclusions¶

Men turned out to be the gender with the most rides, however, its surprising to see that they rather had shorter rides compared to the other genders

Also, Other gender, though it had the least of counts, turned out to be the gender that went for the longest trips

The longest trips also started on the weekends, this could be as a result of majority of the agegroup being those of the working class so they are mostly availabe for longer trips on weekends
